# Deconstruct Workflow

**Purpose**: Break down any problem, system, or concept into its fundamental constituent parts and irreducible truths.

**When to Use**: Starting first-principles analysis, intractable problems, costs that seem fixed, inherited solutions that feel wrong.

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Subject

State clearly: "We are deconstructing: [subject]" -- a problem, system, cost, or architecture.

### Step 2: List Stated Components

What does everyone say this is made of? List all commonly accepted parts without judgment. What does the market/industry say? What do stakeholders assume?

### Step 3: Go Deeper -- Actual Constituents

For each component ask: "What is THIS actually made of?"
- What are the material/physical constituents?
- What are the actual inputs required?
- What is the minimum viable version?
- If we bought raw materials, what would they cost?

### Step 4: Identify Fundamental Truths

What remains when you cannot decompose further?

**Fundamental**: laws of physics, mathematical certainties, empirically verified facts.
**NOT fundamental**: best practices, "how it's always been done," market prices, conventional wisdom.

### Step 5: Map the Gap

| Stated Component | Actual Constituents | Gap/Insight |
|------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| [Market view] | [Fundamental parts] | [What we learned] |

## Output Template

```markdown
## Deconstruction: [Subject]

### What We're Told
[Common description]

### Stated Components
1. [Component 1]  2. [Component 2]

### Actual Constituents
**[Component 1]**: Parts: [list] | Real cost: [amount] | Insight: [difference]

### Fundamental Truths (Irreducible)
1. [Truth -- cannot be decomposed further]

### Key Gaps
| Stated | Actual | Gap |
|--------|--------|-----|
| [X costs $Y] | [Materials cost $Z] | [Difference is not fundamental] |
```

## After Deconstruct

Flow to **Challenge** (question each constraint) or **Reconstruct** (build optimal solution from truths).
